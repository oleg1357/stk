STKHB Supply Controls

Controlling Introduction and Removal of Supply Is Essential to a Healthy Token Ecosystem.

Incentives, Growth, Deflationary Mechanisms

Supply Hard Cap's & STKHB


STKHB is considered to be an "inflationary token" due to no supply hard cap in the contract .

Why does STKHB not have a hard cap? -

There were several considerations that went into not introducing a hard cap on STKHB supply. Incentives, Growth, Deflationary Mechanisms. STKHB's main goal is to provide incentives to stake tokens and coins to grow a users portfolio value. The value of a users portfolio will falter to grow if there are no deflationary mechanisms to control new supply introduction vs. total circulating supply. Thus, STKHB provides growth incentives to users while providing additional growth incentives that have deflationary mechanisms built in. The goal of the deflationary mechanisms is to at least limit new supply introduction and ideally, deflate the total supply of STKHB. 

Incentives & Growth -


Liquidity, Trade Activity, Stake Activity

Liquidity - 

One of the top priorities in a healthy token ecosystem is liquidity provision. Adding liquidity to STKHB markets is a great way to support the project, increase market stability & potentially increase your earnings over HODLing and staking. Before adding liquidity to STKHB markets, familirize yourself with the risks and DYOR into liquidity provision. 

Trade Activity -

With the contract design of STKHB, the strongest initial incentive a user may see is the potential earnings from staking. While holding and staking bear no principal token risk, those tokens are removed from circulation and burned during the process. This leaves the active trading market with less tokens to trade on, potentially creating a bottleneck for new users (While this is extremely unlikely, it is a possibility). Liquidity provision is the easiest soultion to this issue.

Stake Activity - 

Staking reduces the total circulation and total supply of STKHB in the initial contract release. When tokens are staked, they are burned. When a user removes their stake, their tokens are re-minted and remitted back into the circulating supply. Staking rewards are redeemable at any time and also add to the circulating supply of STKHB. The trick is to balance users growth vs. total supply inflation or deflation. When there's a large influx of new users, it is important that their is supply available to purchase. Conversley, when users numbers are depreciating, so should supply. 

Deflationary Mechanisms -


Inflationary Mechanisms Vs. Deflationary Mechanisms

Inflationary Mechanisms -  

New STKHB supply is only introduced by one mechanism, staking. Dev's do not have the ability to mint tokens at will, deflating supply and potentially doing some nasty things we have seen some not so savory project developers do to their user base. 

The maximum daily introduction of new tokens can be calculated by:

((true total supply * max fixed APY)/365) = maximum daily supply introduction 

Looking deeper into this equation, it is very unlikely that the true total supply will be staked at any time. True total supply is equal to:

(staked tokens (which are burned upon staking) + total supply (true supply - staked tokens))

 For the true total supply to be staked it would mean that there are 0 tokens in circulation. 

The actual daily introduction of supply is already calculated for you and displayed on Stake Hub's application but the formula is simple if you'd like to calculate it:

(staked tokens *  APY) / 365  = actual daily supply introduction 

Staking and voting ignore decimal places. Only whole tokens may be staked or voted. Decimals of tokens are tradeable and transferable.

Deflationary Mechanisms - 

Since the total daily introduction of STKB supply is dynamic and will not be the same from one day to another, it is important that our deflationary mechanisms follow a similar course. There are currently two built in deflationary mechanisms in the STKHB contract. 

STAKING FEE - There is a 0.5% fee to create a new stake. This fee is burned instantly and is non-recoverable. (I.E 1000 tokens are submitted to a new stake, 995 tokens will be credited to the total stake amount and 5 tokens will be permanently burned). 

BURN VOTING -  StakeHub offers two voting methods, non-burn and burn voting. Burn voting proposals will alter the state of contract constants. Any vote submitted to burn voting will burn an equal amount of STKHB from a users wallet. (I.E A user submits a burn vote of 10,000 STKHB, 10,000 STKHB will be burned from the users wallet and is non-recoverable).   

Additional mechanisms that will be used to deflate STKHB supply will be determined by one of our first STKHB stake holder votes. Regardless of the mechanisms that are chosen to be implemented for deflationary techniques, target burn rates will be consistent with actual supply growth rates. The idea is to allow minimal supply inflation while striving to actually deflate the total supply of STKHB. Minor supply inflation is a non issue as it does allow user growth on the platform. Rampant inflation however, is an issue.

The deflationary mechanisms instituted on the platform will utilize a very similar calculation to determine the minimum amount of STKHB thats needs to be burned. (new daily supply introduction = new daily burn goal). Since both the inflation and deflation of supply will be dynamic there will be periods of supply inflation and conversley, deflation. 

There will be a maximum deflation threshold that is implemented. For instance, STKHB was issued with a total true supply of 1b tokens. Even though there is a new introduction of supply, the deflationary mechanisms in place have deflated the total true supply to 900mm tokens. At this point the tokens that would have been burned will instead be shifted into a new inflationary mechanism with a smaller deflationary aspect (a raffle for instance(subject to STKHB stake holder voting)). The actual maximum threshold for deflation is yet to be determined and very well may be dynamic.
